## 1. Repeated Hiring Signals

Across Microsoft, Amazon, and Apple, the strongest recurring requirements are:

1. End-to-end design ownership
   - Discovery, ideation, UX, UI, prototyping, execution, iteration, and launch.
2. Portfolio proof
   - Portfolio must show shipped work, design process, problem solving, hands-on craft, and clear communication.
3. Interaction and visual design craft
   - Clear flows, high-fidelity UI, strong typography/layout/color judgment, and attention to detail.
4. Prototyping
   - Low- to high-fidelity prototypes, functional or testable artifacts, and rapid exploration.
5. Ambiguity and complex systems
   - Ability to turn unclear requirements or complex workflows into simple, usable product experiences.
6. Design systems and scalability
   - Components, patterns, guidelines, platform conventions, and consistency across products.
7. Cross-functional collaboration
   - Close work with PMs, engineers, researchers, data science, content design, stakeholders, and leadership.
8. Research and data-informed decisions
   - Customer insight, usability principles, qualitative/quantitative data, telemetry, success criteria, and experimentation.
9. Storytelling and presentation
   - Ability to explain decisions, defend design direction, and align stakeholders through strong narratives.
10. AI and emerging tools
   - AI-assisted workflows, AI-native experiences, data-heavy interfaces, or intelligent product systems.
11. Accessibility and inclusive design
   - Inclusive principles, accessibility standards, usability, and responsible design.
12. Delivery realism
   - Engineering feasibility, implementation constraints, design specs, handoff, and shipped outcomes.

### Case Study

Big Tech reviewers need to see both craft and thinking quickly. Case studies should use this sequence:

1. TL;DR
   - Role, platform, timeline, team, impact, and 1 sentence result.
2. Final Design Preview
   - Best screens, flow, prototype clip, or design system artifact.
3. Problem & Context
   - User problem, business problem, constraints, ambiguity.
4. My Role & Ownership
   - Hands-on design contribution first.
   - PM/product ownership second.
5. Research & Inputs
   - User insight, stakeholder input, data, competitive review, constraints.
6. Design Strategy
   - Design principles, prioritization, success criteria.
7. UX Structure
   - IA, journey map, user flow, task flow, system map.
8. Exploration & Iteration
   - Sketches, wireframes, options considered, tradeoffs.
9. High-Fidelity Design
   - Screens, interaction details, visual system, accessibility considerations.
10. Prototype & Validation
   - Prototype type, what was tested, what changed.
11. Design System / Handoff
   - Components, specs, documentation, engineering collaboration.
12. Outcome & Reflection
   - Metrics, launch result, learning, next iteration.

## 5. Portfolio Rubric For Erik

Use this as the evaluation checklist before publishing.

### Craft

- Does each case show polished final UI?
- Are typography, spacing, hierarchy, and interaction details visible?
- Are mobile/web/platform constraints clear?

### Product Thinking

- Is the user problem clearly stated?
- Is the business goal clear?
- Are tradeoffs and prioritization explained?
- Does the story show why decisions were made?

### End-to-End Process

- Is there enough discovery, flow, wireframe, prototype, and final design evidence?
- Does the case avoid becoming only a final screenshot gallery?

### Research / Data

- Are user insights, metrics, or validation steps included?
- If metrics are unavailable, are qualitative outcomes and constraints explained honestly?

### Systems

- Are components, patterns, guidelines, accessibility, or documentation visible?
- Is scalability part of the design story?

### Collaboration

- Does the case show work with PM, engineering, researchers, clients, and stakeholders?
- Does PM experience strengthen the designer story rather than replace it?

### Storytelling

- Can a reviewer understand the project in 3 minutes?
- Is there a clear narrative from problem to outcome?
- Are visuals doing enough work?
